Outreach

Wisconsin Prison Humanities Project

The Wisconsin Prison Humanities Project, formerly the Oakhill Humanities Project, is a volunteer program based out the University of Wisconsin -- Madison with the mission of providing a range of college-level courses to inmates in Wisconsin prisons.

I was invited to design and teach a new pilot course for the program, "Introduction to Digital Literacy", which would teach students how to use computers, mobile technology, and the internet in safe and productive ways. Click below to see the full course materials from out first semester.

Our first semester recently concluded successfully with a fully-enrolled class of 15 reporting that their apprehensiveness with computers has diminished and their comfort and excitement for technology has improved. We are excited to announce our return for the 2023 summer and fall terms with an expanded volunteer base and an anticipated full enrollment. Click below to learn more about the Wisconsin Prison Humanities Project and get the latest news on our courses.

Dallas-Fort Worth Tap Talks

Founded by Dr. Leslie Rogers, DFW Tap Talks invites the public to attend fascinating and hilarious science lectures from local scientists, hosted by brewery partners in the DFW area. Topics range from quantum mechanics to paleontology, with the occasional labrador raffle assistant. In 2018, I gave a talk on the science of recycling and geopolitical nature of the industry.

UTA Particle Physics Summer Camp

Every year, the Neutrino Rare Event Searches (νRES) group at the University of Texas at Arlington (UTA) hosts a 6-week summer camp for high school juniors and seniors to get the full experience of being a particle physicist.

Students attend lectures by our particle physics faculty, tour our labs with our research staff, and learn how to analyze real CERN particle data with Python to discover the Higgs Boson. To find out when our next session is, click below.
